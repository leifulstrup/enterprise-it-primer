# Disclaimer & Methodology

## AI-Assisted Content Disclosure

This **Enterprise IT Fundamentals Primer** was developed with significant assistance from artificial intelligence tools, specifically Anthropic's Claude, under the direction and editorial oversight of the course instructor at the AU Kogod School of Business.

The site is provided as a **supplementary educational resource** for students enrolled in ITEC-617 "Information and Technology." It is not a substitute for course materials, textbook readings, peer-reviewed publications, or authoritative industry sources.

---

## What "AI-Assisted" Means in This Context

The content on this site was produced through an iterative human-AI collaboration:

1. **Topic selection and structure** were determined by the instructor based on ITEC-617 learning objectives and MBA curriculum requirements.
2. **Draft content** was generated by AI based on instructor-provided outlines, prompts, and source references.
3. **Editorial review** was conducted by the instructor, who revised, corrected, expanded, and restructured AI-generated drafts.
4. **Data points and citations** reference published industry sources (e.g., Gartner, IDC, McKinsey, Deloitte). Where specific figures are cited, the original source is noted inline. However, AI may have rounded, extrapolated, or synthesized data in ways that introduce inaccuracies.
5. **Case studies** labeled as "illustrative composites" are not accounts of specific named organizations. They are constructed from common industry patterns documented in published benchmarking studies to illustrate analytical concepts.

## How to Use This Resource Responsibly

!!! warning "Important Guidance"

    - **Do not cite this primer as a primary source** in academic papers, business reports, or professional work. Instead, use it to identify relevant topics and then consult the original sources cited on each page.
    - **Verify data before making business decisions.** Industry statistics, benchmarks, and trend data should be confirmed against the original publications (Gartner, IDC, Deloitte, etc.) before being used in professional contexts.
    - **Treat case studies as teaching tools, not factual accounts.** They illustrate analytical patterns, not specific organizational experiences.
    - **Check external links.** URLs to third-party resources may change or become outdated over time.
