# UNC Kenan-Flagler Business School -- Analytics/Technology Course Inventory

## Program Overview

UNC Kenan-Flagler has made a significant investment in analytics and technology education, offering two relevant concentrations within the MBA:

- **Business Analytics & Management Science** -- one of the most extensive analytics concentrations among peer MBA programs, with 16+ courses spanning statistical methods, machine learning, and domain-specific analytics applications.
- **Technology Innovation** -- focused on digital strategy, platform economics, and technology entrepreneurship.

UNC's distinctive approach is its **industry-specific analytics tracks**, which allow students to apply analytics techniques to particular business domains (marketing, finance, healthcare, operations, supply chain).

## Observations

- UNC Kenan-Flagler's analytics offering is **the broadest among the programs surveyed** in terms of number of dedicated analytics courses.
- The **industry-specific analytics tracks** are a differentiator -- most peer programs teach analytics methods generically, while UNC connects methods to domain problems.
- Strong **technical literacy** component: Python, SQL, and data visualization are taught as standalone courses, not just embedded topics.
- **Gap relative to AU Kogod**: UNC does not appear to offer dedicated courses in IT financial management, enterprise architecture, or vendor/outsourcing management -- areas where Kogod's ITEC curriculum is distinctive.
- **Strength relative to AU Kogod**: UNC's domain-specific analytics tracks (marketing, finance, healthcare, supply chain) are more extensive. The primer could incorporate brief "analytics in practice" sections to bridge this gap.
- UNC's approach suggests that **analytics fundamentals** and **data visualization** deserve prominent treatment in the primer, as these are universally covered across top programs.
