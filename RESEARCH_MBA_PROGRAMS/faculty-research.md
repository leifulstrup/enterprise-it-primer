## Faculty Profiles

### William DeLone (Professor Emeritus)

**Research focus**: Information systems success measurement

**Citation count**: 55,929 (Google Scholar)

**Seminal contribution**: The **DeLone & McLean IS Success Model** (1992, updated 2003)

The DeLone & McLean model is one of the most cited frameworks in the information systems discipline. It provides a comprehensive taxonomy of IS success with six interdependent dimensions:

```
System Quality ──────┐
                     ├──→ Use ──────────┐
Information Quality ─┤                  ├──→ Net Benefits
                     ├──→ User          │
Service Quality ─────┘    Satisfaction ──┘
```

**Model dimensions:**

| Dimension | Definition | Example Measures |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| System Quality | Technical performance of the IS | Reliability, response time, usability, flexibility |
| Information Quality | Quality of output produced by the IS | Accuracy, completeness, timeliness, relevance |
| Service Quality | Quality of IT support and service | Responsiveness, assurance, empathy (adapted from SERVQUAL) |
| Use | Degree and manner of IS utilization | Frequency of use, nature of use, extent of use |
| User Satisfaction | User's level of satisfaction with the IS | Overall satisfaction, information satisfaction, system satisfaction |
| Net Benefits | Net impact of the IS on stakeholders | Productivity, cost reduction, decision quality, organizational impact |

**Key publications:**

- DeLone, W.H. & McLean, E.R. (1992). "Information Systems Success: The Quest for the Dependent Variable." *Information Systems Research*, 3(1), 60-95.
- DeLone, W.H. & McLean, E.R. (2003). "The DeLone and McLean Model of Information Systems Success: A Ten-Year Update." *Journal of Management Information Systems*, 19(4), 9-30.

**Primer relevance**: The D&M model is referenced in the IT governance and enterprise applications sections. It provides students with a structured way to evaluate whether an IT system is delivering value -- a fundamental management question.

### Erran Carmel (Professor)

**Research focus**: Global software development, nearshoring, IT offshoring

**Citation count**: 10,738 (Google Scholar)

**Key contributions:**

- **Nearshoring framework**: Analysis of the shift from offshoring to nearshoring in IT services, examining factors like time zone overlap, cultural proximity, and travel convenience. Prescient work that anticipated the post-COVID restructuring of global IT delivery.
- **Global software development**: Pioneering research on "follow-the-sun" development, distributed teams, and the challenges of coordinating software work across geographies and cultures.
- **IT nations taxonomy**: Classification of countries by their role in the global IT services ecosystem (mature, emerging, aspiring).

**Key publications:**

- Carmel, E. & Abbott, P. (2007). "Why 'Nearshore' Means That Distance Matters." *Communications of the ACM*, 50(10), 40-46.
- Carmel, E. & Tjia, P. (2005). *Offshoring Information Technology: Sourcing and Outsourcing to a Global Workforce*. Cambridge University Press.
- Carmel, E. (1999). *Global Software Teams: Collaborating Across Borders and Time Zones*. Prentice Hall.

**Primer relevance**: Directly relevant to the vendor management and outsourcing sections. Carmel's nearshoring framework helps students understand the strategic considerations behind IT sourcing decisions -- a topic uniquely strong in Kogod's curriculum.

### Gwanhoo Lee (Professor; Department Chair)

**Research focus**: Digital transformation, AI governance, software agility, open government

**Citation count**: 6,421 (Google Scholar)

**Key contributions:**

- **Open government maturity model**: Framework for assessing government digital openness and data transparency. Influential in public sector IT governance research.
- **Software agility research**: Analysis of how organizations achieve agility in software development and IT operations; factors that enable or inhibit agile adoption.
- **Digital transformation governance**: Research on how organizations govern digital transformation initiatives, including the role of the CDO and cross-functional digital teams.
- **AI governance**: Emerging research on frameworks for governing AI adoption in enterprises, including risk assessment and ethical considerations.

**Key publications:**

- Lee, G. & Kwak, Y.H. (2012). "An Open Government Maturity Model for Social Media-Based Public Engagement." *Government Information Quarterly*, 29(4), 492-503.
- Lee, G. & Xia, W. (2010). "Toward Agile: An Integrated Analysis of Quantitative and Qualitative Field Data on Software Development Agility." *MIS Quarterly*, 34(1), 87-114.

**Primer relevance**: Lee's work connects to the digital transformation, AI governance, and agile methodology content in the primer. His open government research also provides a public sector perspective that broadens the enterprise IT discussion beyond private sector contexts.

## Recommendations

1. **Strengthen D&M model coverage**: The DeLone & McLean model is Kogod's most significant intellectual contribution to the IS field. The primer should feature it prominently, ideally with an interactive diagram and worked example.

2. **Add Carmel's nearshoring framework**: The vendor management section should explicitly reference Carmel's work on nearshoring factors, providing students with a decision framework they can apply to real sourcing decisions.

3. **Incorporate Lee's agility and AI governance work**: As the primer expands its AI coverage, Lee's frameworks for AI governance and organizational agility provide Kogod-specific lenses for these topics.

4. **Faculty attribution**: Where primer content draws on faculty research, include brief attribution (e.g., "Based on research by AU Kogod Professor Gwanhoo Lee") to strengthen the connection between the primer and the department's intellectual contributions.

5. **Case study connections**: Faculty research can serve as the basis for discussion questions and case examples in the primer, reinforcing the link between ITEC coursework and active research.
