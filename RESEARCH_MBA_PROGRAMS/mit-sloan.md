# MIT Sloan School of Management -- IT/Analytics Course Inventory

## Program Overview

MIT Sloan integrates technology deeply into its MBA and specialized master's programs. The **Information Technologies (IT)** group within Sloan offers courses on strategic IT management, information economics, and data-driven decision making. MIT's unique strength is the ability for MBA students to cross-register with CSAIL (Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory), EECS (Electrical Engineering and Computer Science), and other departments across the Institute.

Sloan also offers the **Master of Business Analytics (MBAn)**, a joint program with the Operations Research Center, which is one of the premier analytics-focused graduate degrees in the country.

## Key IT-Related Courses

| Course | Title | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 15.320 | Strategic IT Management | IT strategy alignment, governance, digital transformation, and technology investment decisions. |
| 15.561 | Information Technology Essentials | Foundational IT course; infrastructure, enterprise systems, cloud, security. Closest parallel to AU's ITEC-610. |
| 15.563J | Data, Models, and Decisions | Statistical modeling, simulation, optimization for business decisions. Cross-listed with Engineering. |
| 15.567 | Economics of Information: Strategy, Structure, and Pricing | Information goods pricing, network effects, platform economics, intellectual property strategy. |
| 15.580 | Seminar in IT Management | Research seminar exploring current topics in IT management; faculty-led deep dives. |

## Master of Business Analytics (MBAn)

The MBAn is a 12-month program operated jointly by Sloan and the Operations Research Center:

- **Core**: Machine learning, optimization, applied probability, analytics edge, analytics capstone
- **Electives**: Deep learning, causal inference, computational statistics, operations analytics
- **Capstone**: Corporate-sponsored analytics project (7-month engagement)
- **Placement**: Strong placement in tech, consulting, and financial services

## Cross-Registration Opportunities

MIT MBA students can take courses across the Institute, including:

- **CSAIL**: AI, machine learning, NLP, computer vision, robotics
- **EECS**: Systems engineering, cybersecurity, distributed systems
- **Media Lab**: Innovation, design thinking, emerging technologies
- **Institute for Data, Systems, and Society (IDSS)**: Social systems modeling, data science

## Observations

- MIT's distinguishing feature is **technical depth** -- MBA students can go as deep as they want by cross-registering into engineering and CS courses.
- The MBAn program is a standalone analytics degree, not a concentration within the MBA -- this reflects MIT's commitment to analytics as a discipline.
- The **information economics** course (15.567) covers platform economics and network effects at a level of rigor uncommon in MBA programs.
- Sloan does not have a large standalone IT course catalog because technology is embedded across operations, strategy, and entrepreneurship courses.
- The IT Essentials course (15.561) is the closest peer to AU's ITEC-610 and the primer content.
